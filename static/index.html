Brain Tumor Detection System

Welcome to our Brain Tumor Detection System. This platform uses advanced artificial intelligence to analyze medical images and detect potential brain tumors with high accuracy.

What is brain tumors?

A brain tumor occurs when abnormal cells form within the brain. There are two main types of tumors: cancerous (malignant) tumors and benign tumors. Cancerous tumors can be divided into primary tumors, which start within the brain, and secondary tumors, which have spread from elsewhere, known as brain metastasis tumors.

Symptoms Guidance

Common Symptoms:

  • Persistent headaches that worsen over time
  • Unexplained nausea or vomiting
  • Vision problems (blurred or double vision)
  • Seizures (especially with no prior history)
  • Balance or coordination difficulties
  • Speech or hearing problems
  • Personality or behavior changes

When to Seek Immediate Help:

  • Sudden onset of severe symptoms
  • Symptoms that progressively worsen
  • Combination of neurological symptoms (e.g. headache + weakness)
  • Seizures in someone with no epilepsy history

Treatment Methods

  • Surgery: Tumor removal when accessible
  • Radiation therapy: Targeted high-energy beams
  • Chemotherapy: Anti-cancer drugs
  • Targeted therapy: Drugs targeting specific tumor features
  • Immunotherapy: Boosting the immune system
  • Supportive care: Managing symptoms and side effects

Patient Support

  • Join brain tumor support groups (online or local)
  • Maintain a symptom diary for doctor visits
  • Ask about clinical trial opportunities
  • Prioritize mental health with counseling if needed
  • For caregivers: Practice active listening and patience

About This Project

This Brain Tumor Detection System was developed using deep learning techniques to analyze medical imaging data. The model was trained on a dataset of MRI scans from:

Kaggle Brain MRI Dataset for Tumor Detection

The segmentation model used in Brainlytics was developed using Python and the U-Net architecture, implemented with PyTorch. It was trained on a dataset of brain MRI scans to accurately detect and segment brain tumors.

Kaggle Brain Tumor Segmentation

The system is intended for research and educational purposes and should not be used as a sole diagnostic tool without consultation with medical professionals.
